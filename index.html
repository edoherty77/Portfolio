Travel Book

Previous Next

With Travel Book, users can easily keep track of the places they have visited, the photos they have taken, and even the miles they have traveled. With the help of Google Maps and Places, and implementing MongoDB, Express, React Native and Node, users can not only check-in to their current location with just the touch of a button, but can also add photos. And since everything for that cool trip you went on is in one place, it's easier to share with your friends.

Vote-ify

Previous Next

Vote-ify brings Democracy to playlists. Using the Spotify API, users can listen to and contribute songs to 12 playlists, all curated and managed by the community. Carefully creating a Spotify API endpoint, I implemented a search bar where a user can search and 'suggest' a song. It is then sent into a queue where it has two weeks to earn enough votes to be officially added to the playlist.

Let's Chat

Previous Next

As a GA student, I frequently used Slack as a means of communication and education. Our main channel was populated with useful information, but due to higher levels of activity, finding the information you needed became difficult, even with Slack's search feature. Let's Chat was created to alleviate this issue by requiring users to select a tag relevant to the topic they are posting. Using Express and MongoDB, I implemented a search feature where users can sort posts by selecting their desired tag.

About Me

I eat at all hours of the day and night (I will literally wake up at 6 AM for a snack and go back to sleep). I work hard, play hard, but most of all, I code hard.

Once I returned, I considered getting my teaching degree or going into business with my father, a renowned chef, but was soon introduced to coding. I immediately realized my calling. I continued to work under my father as I got serious about coding, taking courses and expanding my expertise.
